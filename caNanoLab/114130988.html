Description: Mycoplasma is a microorganism that lacks a nucleus and a cell wall which makes it unaffected by many antibiotics. Nanoparticles submitted to the NCL may be subjected to testing for mycoplasma when deemed necessary. The types of nanoparticle formulations generally tested for mycoplasma contamination include those that incorporate a component derived from an animal or hybridoma cultures.
