NCL Method IEA-1: Analysis of Nanoparticle Effects on Invasion of Cancer Cells

This document provides a protocol for a real-time, quantitative assessment of nanoparticle-mediated inhibition of cancer cell invasion. This assay uses human breast cancer cells, MDA-MB-231, as a model cell line. The cells are separated from control chemoattractant and test-nanoparticles by an 8 um filter and an extracellular matrix. The cell migration through the filter is then monitored using a label-free technology developed by ACEA Biosciences, wherein cell attachment to gold electrodes on the underside of the filter results in a change in the impedance. This is subsequently converted into the cell index, which is proportional to the number of cells migrated through the filter.
